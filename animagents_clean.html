01

Abstract

Animation pre-production transforms early concepts into a coherent blueprint across interdependent stages such as ideation, scripting, design, and storyboarding. Although many professionals use generative AI tools for early exploration and pitching, our formative study with 12 animation professionals revealed key challenges: creators struggle to coordinate fragmented outputs across stages, manage large volumes of content, and maintain continuity and creative control as ideas evolve. To address this gap, we present AnimAgents, a human–AI collaborative system powered by a multi-agent architecture that introduces a stage-aware interaction paradigm for animation pre-production. The system integrates stage-specific workspaces with element-level manipulation and refinement, enabling creators to organize outputs, revisit prior decisions, and carry evolving content across stages in a controllable and traceable workflow. In a within-subjects study with 16 professionals, AnimAgents significantly outperformed a strong single-agent baseline in coordination, consistency, and overall satisfaction (p < .01). A field deployment with four creators further demonstrated its effectiveness in real-world projects.

02

Formative Study Findings

We recruited 12 participants (1–16 YoE, Mean = 7.6): 6 creative directors from three animation studios (P1–P6) and 6 independent animators (P7–P12), with 1.5–2 hour semi-structured interviews covering pre-production workflows, GenAI use, and pain points.

⚡
Information Overload
Participants struggled to manage large volumes of visual and textual material across stages. AI-generated outputs were frequently "mediocre," "irrelevant," or "overly generic," requiring substantial filtering effort.
"There's so much output it's really hard to sort through." — P3
🔗
Continuity Breakdown
Cross-stage continuity was severely undermined by fragmented tools. Earlier decisions were easily lost and GenAI outputs drifted from original story directions over time.
"Even when I asked ChatGPT to generate prompts in the same style, the images from MidJourney still didn't match." — P12
📋
Need for Project Management
Participants wanted GenAI to act as a workflow partner — helping coordinate tasks, structure outputs, and support project management throughout the process, not just generate ideas.
"What we lack most isn't more creative ideas. It's planning, organizing information, and telling me what's still missing." — P7
Design Goals Derived from Findings
DG1 — Continuity & Traceability
Help creators organize outputs, track progress, revisit earlier decisions, and maintain narrative and visual consistency across the full workflow.
DG2 — Structured Visualization
Offer stage-specific visualizations that help creators navigate, compare, and communicate evolving outputs aligned with existing artistic workflows.
DG3 — Agency Preservation
Assist with idea expansion and refinement while reducing low-value overhead, enabling creators to control how AI participates in the process.
03

System Architecture

AnimAgents organizes work into five stages with a centralized Core Agent coordinating four Specialized Agents. Stage-specific boards serve as persistent project memory, and lineage-aware blocks preserve revisions and branches over time.

Cross-Stage Workflow — Non-Linear Navigation
Creators can jump to any stage at any time
Planning
Core Agent records project-level intent, type, genre, audience, and goals. Coordinates all downstream stage activity.
Ideation
Logline, story concept, world concept, character concepts, style description, hero images. Supports divergent alternatives.
Scripting
Structure mapping, story outline, scene list, styleframes. Transitions from divergent to convergent narrative form.
Design → Storyboard
Character & environment sheets (Design), then shot lists and storyboard frames (Storyboard). Final pre-production output.
Specialized Agents — Roles & Responsibilities
Agent Role Description Primary Tasks
Core Agent
Coordinator
 Acts as project manager; interprets user intent, routes tasks to specialized agents, and validates outputs before publishing to boards. Maintains cross-stage project memory. Task routing, memory management, stage switching, output validation, user communication.
Ideation Agent
Divergent
 Divergent and generative; explores multiple narrative directions and expands creative possibilities during early stages. Story ideas, character concepts, world settings, narrative themes, style descriptions.
Scripting Agent
Convergent
 Convergent and structured; transforms abstract concepts into coherent narrative sequences and shot-level breakdowns. Structure mapping, story outlines, scene lists, full scripts, shot lists for storyboarding.
Design Agent
Visual
 Visual and style-driven; transforms concept descriptions into concrete visual design materials with narrative coherence. Character design sheets, environment designs; maintains visual coherence with narrative intent.
Art Agent
Illustrative
 Illustrative and compositional; translates scripts and designs into polished visual assets for storyboards and styleframes. Hero images, styleframes, storyboard frames; aligns visual composition with narrative and design requirements.
Cross-Stage Memory
Outputs from one stage remain available as structured context. Core Agent tracks approved artifacts, revisions, and relationships across boards. Long-term memory via ChromaDB vector store with time-ordered summarized chunks.
Stage-Aware Delegation
The same user request is handled differently depending on active stage. Core Agent encourages divergent alternatives in Ideation, enforces narrative structure in Scripting, checks visual–narrative consistency in Storyboard.
Controlled Publication
Outputs from Specialized Agents are validated by the Core Agent before publishing to the board. Results appear as lineage-aware blocks preserving revisions and branches, ensuring traceability and coherence.
04

Implementation

Runtime & Coordination
  • Built on AutoGen Core 0.6.1 with routed agents and asynchronous orchestration.
  • Python + FastAPI backend with typed SSE events streamed into per-session queues via a dedicated ReplyAgent for real-time UI updates.
  • Next.js frontend with stage-aware spatial boards, lineage-aware block components, and element-level selection.
  • GPT-4.1-mini powers most agent behaviors; GPT-4.1-nano handles lightweight tasks such as memory retrieval.
  • Each request carries a cancellation token; interruptions or failures trigger safe termination and rollback of agent state.
AutoGen 0.6.1 Python FastAPI Next.js SSE GPT-4.1-mini GPT-4.1-nano
Memory & Generation
  • Short-term context: each agent maintains context over recent messages, tool calls, and outputs.
  • Long-term memory (Core Agent only): interactions summarized into time-ordered chunks, indexed in ChromaDB vector store for later retrieval.
  • Flux.1-Kontext-pro for image generation with stage-aware prompting and optional user reference images to preserve style and character consistency.
  • Generated images are stored and embedded directly into blocks on the interface boards.
ChromaDB RAG Flux.1-Kontext-pro Vector Store WebSocket
05

Evaluation Results

A within-subjects study (N=16 professionals, 7-point Likert scale, Wilcoxon signed-rank tests) compared AnimAgents against a strong single-agent baseline using the same AutoGen framework, LLMs, and T2I model with a tailored full-pipeline system prompt. The baseline included parallel image generation and conversational interaction — intentionally stronger than typical industry tools.

RQ1 — Coordination & Continuity
Stage Coordination & Consistency
AnimAgents significantly improved coordination, task progress tracking, and subtask breakdown across stages (p < .01). In the open-ended task, 100% of participants preferred AnimAgents for cross-stage coordination, and 81% preferred it for creative consistency. Many described it as a "project manager" rather than just a consultant.
13% faster task completion (Mean 34.4 vs 39.6 minutes, p < .01)
RQ2 — Organization & Traceability
Output Management & Traceability
Significantly better for keeping content organized and managing information without overload (p < .01, Q6; p < .05, Q7). 94% of participants preferred AnimAgents for output management. Stage-specific boards matched participants' visual-spatial mental models and contrasted sharply with the linear scroll of chat-only tools.
94% participant preference for output management
RQ3 — Agency & Creative Exploration
Controllability & Satisfaction
Rated significantly better for revising outputs (p < .05) and for shaping creative vision rather than automating tasks (p < .05). Element-level selection allowed precise, layer-like editing. Rated significantly higher for reducing mental effort and overall satisfaction (p < .01). Log analysis showed directive interactions rose from 10% to 22%, and revisions dropped from 40% to 30%.
94% participant preference for overall satisfaction
RQ4 — Field Study
Real-World Deployment (1 Week, N=4)
Commercial studio director (P5) reduced storyboard delivery from a week to a day. Original animation director (P24) valued project management but noted AI visuals insufficient for high-standard original work. Both independent animators (P8, P19) valued scaffolding for pre-production stages they typically rushed or skipped. P8 explored agent role-play, assigning director personas to agents for unexpected narrative directions.
4 professionals across commercial studio, original studio, and freelance contexts
"It consolidates each stage's task and systematically places everything from different departments into one place."
P8 — Independent Animator
"From character design to styleframes and storyboards, the style was maintained precisely."
P22 — Independent Animator
"AnimAgents gives me several complete storyboard versions. Even if not every frame was usable, it was more than enough to communicate with clients."
